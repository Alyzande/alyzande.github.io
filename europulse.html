EuroPulse

Real-Time Multilingual Threat Detection and OSINT Analysis in French and German Social Media

Year: 2025

Technologies: Python, Hugging Face Transformers, HDBSCAN, Reddit & Mastodon APIs, Flask, GeoJSON, Real-Time Streaming

Overview

EuroPulse is a real-time multilingual threat detection system designed for open-source intelligence (OSINT) and public-safety monitoring. It detects early reports of violent or dangerous incidents, such as shootings, explosions, riots, or transport attacks, from French and German-language social media posts. The system focuses on posts from independent citizen journalists and eyewitnesses, identifying credible alerts before mainstream media coverage. Its goal is to help analysts and emergency-response teams recognise fast-developing threats across Europe in real time.

Key Features

  • Real-Time Event Detection from platforms such as Reddit (live) and Mastodon or Bluesky (in development).
  • Filters multilingual posts by keywords related to violence, explosions, or emergency response indicators.
  • Performs linguistic normalization and translation across French and German sources.
  • Uses HDBSCAN clustering to group similar reports and highlight emerging incidents geographically.
  • Designed for integration into emergency management dashboards or intelligence fusion centers.

Technical Details

The platform is implemented in Python with modular data collectors for each API. Posts are processed through multilingual NLP pipelines using Hugging Face Transformers models for classification and named-entity recognition. Clustering and noise filtering are achieved with HDBSCAN, and results are visualised on an interactive Leaflet.js or GeoJSON map. Designed for asynchronous real-time operation, the architecture supports event-driven scaling.

Rationale & Purpose

EuroPulse demonstrates how AI and multilingual natural-language processing can enhance early-warning intelligence for public safety. Inspired by incidents such as the Bataclan attack and Christmas market assaults, it seeks to identify the first social-media signals of emerging threats, often minutes before official reports or news coverage. By focusing on citizen journalism and eyewitness data, EuroPulse helps bridge the information gap during rapidly unfolding emergencies.

The project grew from my own experience of refreshing social media feeds during major crises such as the Arab Spring and the Grenfell Tower fire. These moments showed how individuals online can become the first responders in the digital space. This inspired a system capable of amplifying those early, scattered signals into structured intelligence that supports analysts, journalists, and emergency responders in making faster, evidence-based decisions.
